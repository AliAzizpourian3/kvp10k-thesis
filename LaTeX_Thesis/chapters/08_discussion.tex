\section{Limitations and Future Work}
\label{sec:limitations}

\subsection{Current Limitations}

\subsubsection{Hardware and Data Constraints}
Our Stage 3 Mistral baseline and Stage 4 LayoutLMv3 implementation operate under two significant constraints that impact result interpretation:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{LoRA rank reduction}: Hardware constraints (40 GB A100 memory) forced us to use LoRA rank $r=4$ compared to estimated $r \geq 16$ in the official KVP10k baseline. While QLoRA with 4-bit quantization is validated to preserve model quality (Dettmers et al., 2023), the reduced rank limits adaptation capacity. This likely contributes to the performance gap versus the published baseline.
    
    \item \textbf{Training data coverage}: Due to inaccessible source PDFs and scanned documents lacking usable text layers (PyMuPDF limitation), our training set comprises only 5,389 of 9,656 unique pages (55.8\%) and test set 581 of 1,051 pages (55.3\%). This reduced coverage provides less training signal than the full KVP10k dataset, further constraining model learning.
\end{itemize}

When comparing our Mistral results (Stage 3, Table~\ref{tab:stage3_stage0_thresholds}) and LayoutLMv3 results (Stage 4) to published baselines, readers should account for these two confounds: LoRA rank and data coverage. The error analysis (Chapter 7, Section~\ref{subsec:per_cluster_analysis}) provides per-cluster breakdown showing that Mistral's failures are driven primarily by hallucination on sparse layouts and weak spatial grounding, not by the rank or data coverage limitations.

\subsubsection{Architectural Constraints}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Limited to single-page documents
    \item Requires pre-OCR for text
    \item Computational cost for large documents
\end{itemize}
